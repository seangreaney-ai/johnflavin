# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Homeowners in Kerry and Munster planning a renovation or new-build fit-out. They care about quality and finish, want something personalised, and are unlikely to buy from a flat-pack retailer. Primary context: browsing on phone or tablet at home, at early decision-making stage — comparing options, building confidence in a craftsman before making contact.

## Product Purpose

A marketing and portfolio website for Wood Interiors by John Flavin — a one-man custom fitted furniture business in Duagh, Co. Kerry. The site showcases completed work, explains services, and drives inbound inquiries. Success means a visitor views the portfolio, gains confidence in John's craft, and makes contact.

## Brand Personality

Craftsman-led, honest, and grounded. Three words: **precise, warm, local**. Voice is straightforward — quality of work speaks first, personality second. No fluff. Copy is descriptive and specific: colour names, finish types, hardware brands.

## Anti-references

Flat-pack retailer websites (IKEA, Homebase). Generic "modern interiors" templates that look like every renovation brand. Overly corporate or polished — this is a one-man trade, not a franchise.

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work speak.** Imagery and project detail carry weight; copy supports rather than explains.
2. **Earned confidence, not claimed.** Trust comes from specificity — material names, hardware brands, process — not superlatives.
3. **Local and personal first.** Every choice should feel like it belongs in Kerry, not a generic global market.
4. **Detail is the differentiator.** The Blum hinges, the Farrow & Ball finish, the quartz supplier — specificity is a brand signal.
5. **Direct, never fussy.** Navigation, copy, and layout should be clear and purposeful.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Audience skews toward older homeowners; legibility and clarity are priorities.
